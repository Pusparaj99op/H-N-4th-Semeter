Practical No. 02 – Network Cable Crimping (RJ-45)

Aim

To crimp RJ-45 Straight-Through and Cross-Over network cables using the T568A and T568B wiring standards, and to test them using a cable tester.

Theory

UTP (Unshielded Twisted Pair) Cable: The most commonly used cable in Ethernet LANs. It contains 4 pairs of colour-coded twisted wires. The twisting reduces electromagnetic interference (EMI). Common categories: CAT5, CAT5e, CAT6, CAT6a.

RJ-45 Connector: An 8-position, 8-contact (8P8C) modular connector used to terminate UTP Ethernet cables. The pin numbering is 1 to 8 from left to right when holding the connector with the tab facing down and the gold contacts facing towards you.

Wiring Standards (EIA/TIA 568):

Pin T568A (Wire Colour) T568B (Wire Colour)
1White / GreenWhite / Orange
2GreenOrange
3White / OrangeWhite / Green
4BlueBlue
5White / BlueWhite / Blue
6OrangeGreen
7White / BrownWhite / Brown
8BrownBrown

Types of Patch Cables:

  • Straight-Through Cable: Both ends follow the same wiring standard (T568B – T568B). Used to connect unlike devices: PC to Switch, PC to Hub, Router to Switch.
  • Cross-Over Cable: One end is T568A and the other end is T568B (pins 1&2 and 3&6 are crossed). Used to connect like devices: PC to PC, Switch to Switch, Hub to Hub.

Note: Modern network devices support Auto-MDI/MDI-X, which automatically detects the cable type and adjusts accordingly, making crossover cables less commonly needed today.

Apparatus / Requirements

  • CAT5e or CAT6 UTP Cable – approximately 1.5 metres per cable
  • RJ-45 Connectors – minimum 4 pieces
  • RJ-45 Crimping Tool
  • Wire Stripper / Cable Cutter
  • Cable Tester (LAN cable tester / RJ-45 tester)
  • Scale / Ruler (for measuring strip length)

Procedure

A. Straight-Through Cable (T568B – T568B):

  1. Cut approximately 1.5 m of UTP cable using the cutter.
  2. Use the cable stripper to carefully remove about 3–4 cm of the outer plastic jacket from both ends. Avoid nicking the inner wire insulation.
  3. Separate all 4 pairs and untwist each pair of wires.
  4. Arrange and hold the 8 wires in the T568B order (left to right):
    W/Orange – Orange – W/Green – Blue – W/Blue – Green – W/Brown – Brown
  5. Hold the wires firmly between thumb and forefinger. Using the cutter, trim all 8 wires flat to about 12–14 mm from the jacket.
  6. Hold the RJ-45 connector with the tab facing downward and carefully slide all 8 wires into the correct channels, ensuring each wire reaches the far end of the connector.
  7. Place the connector firmly into the crimping tool and squeeze the handles fully until you hear a click.
  8. Repeat steps 3–7 for the second end, using the same T568B order.

B. Cross-Over Cable (T568A – T568B):

  1. Prepare a fresh piece of cable as above.
  2. For End 1: arrange and crimp wires in T568A order:
    W/Green – Green – W/Orange – Blue – W/Blue – Orange – W/Brown – Brown
  3. For End 2: arrange and crimp wires in T568B order (same as straight-through).

C. Testing with Cable Tester:

  1. Insert one end of the completed cable into the "Main" port of the cable tester, and the other end into the "Remote" port.
  2. Turn the tester ON.
  3. For a Straight-Through cable: LEDs on both units should light up in matching sequence — 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8 simultaneously.
  4. For a Cross-Over cable: LEDs light in a crossed pattern — Main LED 1 matches Remote LED 3; Main LED 2 matches Remote LED 6, and vice versa.
  5. If any LED does not light, there is a wiring fault. Re-terminate that end and re-test.

Result

RJ-45 Straight-Through and Cross-Over cables were successfully crimped following the T568B and T568A wiring standards respectively. The cable tester confirmed proper continuity and correct pin-to-pin mapping for both cables. The Straight-Through cable lit LEDs in sequence 1–8, and the Cross-Over cable showed the correct crossed pattern (1↔3, 2↔6).
